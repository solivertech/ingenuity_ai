# Toyota Grand Highlander Hybrid — Carvana Listing Evaluation Context

> **Purpose:** This document provides authoritative reference data for evaluating used Carvana
> listings for the Toyota Grand Highlander Hybrid. Use this data to assess value, identify trim
> levels, flag reliability concerns, and contextualize pricing.
>
> **Search parameters:**
> - Make/Model: Toyota Grand Highlander Hybrid
> - Years: 2024–2025 (only years this model has existed)
> - Mileage: Under 50,000 miles
> - Excluded: Hybrid MAX trim — do not recommend or highlight any MAX trim listing
> - Powertrain: Standard hybrid only (2.5L, 245 hp) — not the turbocharged Hybrid MAX

---

## Part 1 — Model Overview

The Toyota Grand Highlander is a three-row midsize SUV that debuted as a 2024 model. It is
positioned between the smaller Toyota Highlander and the larger Sequoia. The Grand Highlander
is notably longer and taller than the standard Highlander, with most of the added size going to
a genuinely adult-usable third row and substantially more cargo space.

**Key facts:**
- First model year: 2024. There are no pre-2024 Grand Highlander models — any listing showing an
  earlier year is a different (standard) Highlander and should be flagged and excluded.
- The vehicle is sold in two distinct powertrain families: standard Hybrid and Hybrid MAX. These
  are fundamentally different vehicles in price, performance, and fuel economy. This search is
  exclusively for the standard Hybrid (non-MAX).
- The Lexus TX is the luxury-branded equivalent of the Grand Highlander on the same platform.

### Standard Hybrid powertrain (in-scope)

- Engine: 2.5L naturally aspirated 4-cylinder + two electric motors
- Combined HP: 245
- Transmission: CVT (eCVT)
- AWD: Optional (Electronic On-Demand AWD) on XLE | Standard on Limited and Nightshade
- Towing capacity: 3,500 lbs
- Fuel tank: 17.83 gallons | Uses 87 octane (regular unleaded)

### Hybrid MAX powertrain (OUT OF SCOPE — exclude all MAX listings)

- Engine: Turbocharged 2.4L 4-cylinder + electric motors
- Combined HP: 362
- Transmission: 6-speed automatic (not CVT)
- AWD: Standard
- Towing capacity: 5,000 lbs
- MPG: 26–27 combined — significantly worse than standard hybrid
- Available on: Limited (optional) and Platinum (standard only)
- Price premium: $4,000–5,000 over equivalent non-MAX trim

**Identification rule:** Any listing with "MAX" in the trim name, or with a 2.4L turbo engine
noted, or with 362 hp, is a Hybrid MAX and must be flagged as out of scope regardless of price.

## Part 2 — Reliability & Known Issues

This section is critical for a model this new. The Grand Highlander has a notable pattern of
first-year issues that should inform how listings are evaluated.

### Reliability summary

Toyota has an excellent long-term reliability reputation, but the Grand Highlander has not fully
lived up to it so far. The 2024 model year has the most documented issues. The 2025 model year
appears to have addressed several concerns, though long-term data is still accumulating for both
years given how new the model is.

### Known issues — 2024 model year (flag in analysis)

- **Hood vibration at highway speeds:** A widely reported complaint. Toyota issued a technical
  service bulletin but some owners report the fix is incomplete.
- **Fuel tank underfilling:** A significant issue documented by multiple owners. The tank stops
  accepting fuel well short of its 17.83-gallon capacity, sometimes filling only 10–11 gallons.
  Toyota acknowledged the issue but as of 2024–2025 had not issued a final fix. This directly
  affects real-world range.
- **Cabin noise and rattles:** Multiple owner reports of squeaks, rattles, and wind noise.
  Particularly noted on the Platinum trim with its larger glass areas.
- **Clicking/noise when turning:** Some owners reported a CV joint or axle-related noise when
  turning. Toyota replaced driver-side axles in documented cases.
- **Warning light clusters:** Several owners reported dashboard warning light events (AWD
  malfunction, drive control malfunction) appearing in the first few thousand miles.
- **Side airbag recall (2024):** A recall was issued for 2024 Grand Highlander models for side
  airbags that may not inflate properly if a window is open. Verify recall completion status on
  any 2024 listing via VIN check at nhtsa.gov.
- **Fuel door misalignment:** Fuel door hitting the body panel and wearing paint. Toyota
  acknowledged a redesign was required.
- **Inaccurate fuel gauge readings:** Separate from the underfilling issue; gauge readings
  reported as inaccurate by some owners.

### 2025 model year

The 2025 model addresses some first-year concerns and introduced two new trims (LE and Hybrid
Nightshade Edition). Early owner feedback is more positive than 2024, and the airbag recall
does not apply. However, given the recency of the model, there is still limited long-term
reliability data. Treat any 2025 listing as newer-generation but unproven over the long term.

### Practical guidance for evaluation

- Flag any 2024 listing with a note that it is the first model year with documented early issues
- Recommend verifying the side airbag recall has been completed on 2024 VINs
- A 2025 listing with under 20,000 miles represents the lower-risk option of the two years
- Do not penalize 2025 listings for the 2024 issues — they are different model years

## Part 3 — Trim Levels (Standard Hybrid Only)

### 2024 Hybrid trim lineup

Three hybrid trim levels were available for 2024: XLE, Limited, and Hybrid MAX (excluded).
Nightshade was introduced for 2024 as a gas model only — the Hybrid Nightshade came in 2025.

| Trim | Drivetrain | HP | MPG (city/hwy/comb) | MSRP (new, 2024) |
|------|------------|----|--------------------|-------------------|
| Hybrid XLE (FWD) | FWD | 245 | 37 / 34 / 36 | ~$44,670 |
| Hybrid XLE (AWD) | AWD optional | 245 | 36 / 32 / 34 | ~$46,070 |
| Hybrid Limited (AWD) | AWD standard | 245 | 35 / 31 / 33 | ~$51,060 |

### 2025 Hybrid trim lineup

Five hybrid trim levels for 2025 (excluding MAX): LE, XLE, Limited, Nightshade.

| Trim | Drivetrain | HP | MPG (city/hwy/comb) | MSRP (new, 2025) |
|------|------------|----|--------------------|-------------------|
| Hybrid LE (AWD) | AWD standard | 245 | 36 / 32 / 34 | ~$44,210 |
| Hybrid XLE (FWD) | FWD | 245 | 37 / 34 / 36 | ~$45,380 |
| Hybrid XLE (AWD) | AWD optional | 245 | 36 / 32 / 34 | ~$46,780 |
| Hybrid Limited (AWD) | AWD standard | 245 | 36 / 32 / 34 | ~$51,710 |
| Hybrid Nightshade (AWD) | AWD standard | 245 | 36 / 32 / 34 | ~$52,610 |

**Note on real-world MPG:** EPA figures are optimistic for this vehicle. Owner reports from
forums indicate real-world averages of 27–32 MPG in mixed driving, and as low as 24–27 MPG at
sustained highway speeds above 70 mph. This is lower than Toyota's hybrid reputation suggests
and should be factored into value assessment. The fuel tank underfilling issue (2024 models)
further reduces practical range.

## Part 4 — Trim Feature Breakdown

### Hybrid LE (2025 only)

The entry-level trim added for 2025. Well-equipped for the price with AWD standard.

Standard features include:
- 2.5L hybrid + AWD standard
- 12.3" touchscreen infotainment
- 7 USB-C ports
- Toyota Safety Sense 3.0 (adaptive cruise, lane keeping, automatic emergency braking,
  blind-spot warning with rear cross-traffic alert)
- Digital key (smartphone-as-key)
- Power liftgate
- Heated outer mirrors
- 8-passenger capacity available (bench seat option for 2nd row)
- Fabric upholstery (Light Gray or Black)
- 18" alloy wheels

**What it lacks vs. XLE:** Heated front seats, wireless charging, SofTex synthetic leather
upholstery, 2nd-row window sunshades, upgraded audio.

---

### Hybrid XLE ⭐ Best Value Trim

The most commonly recommended trim for used buyers. Available in FWD or AWD.

**FWD vs. AWD decision:** FWD gets 37/34/36 MPG vs. AWD's 36/32/34. For buyers who do not
need AWD capability, FWD delivers slightly better efficiency and will typically cost less on the
used market. AWD adds approximately $1,400 at MSRP; used price gap is typically $1,000–2,000.

Standard features include:
- 2.5L hybrid + FWD standard (AWD optional)
- 12.3" touchscreen infotainment
- Toyota Safety Sense 3.0
- SofTex synthetic leather upholstery (Light Gray or Black)
- Heated front seats
- Wireless phone charger
- 2nd-row window sunshades
- Power tilt/slide moonroof
- 18" silver-painted alloy wheels
- 7- or 8-passenger seating (bench seat option available)
- Smart Key System with push-button start on all doors and liftgate
- JBL premium audio system (11-speaker) — available as option

**What it lacks vs. Limited:** Ventilated front seats, heated 2nd-row outboard seats, leather
upholstery, panoramic surround-view camera, larger JBL audio standard, front/rear parking
sensors with auto braking, memory driver's seat settings.

---

### Hybrid Limited

A meaningful comfort step up from XLE. AWD is standard — FWD is not available.

Standard features (adds over XLE):
- Leather upholstery (Light Gray or Black)
- Ventilated front seats
- Heated outboard 2nd-row seats
- JBL 11-speaker premium audio (standard, not optional)
- Front and rear parking sensors with automatic braking
- Panoramic surround-view camera (360-degree overhead view)
- Memory settings for driver's seat and mirrors
- Power-adjustable driver's seat (10-way)
- Rain-sensing windshield wipers
- Auto-dimming rearview mirror
- 20" alloy wheels

**What it lacks vs. Platinum/Nightshade:** Head-up display, digital rearview mirror, panoramic
roof with power sunshade (panoramic moonroof on XLE is a standard moonroof, not full panoramic).

---

### Hybrid Nightshade Edition (2025 only)

An appearance package built on the Limited's feature base with a blacked-out aesthetic. AWD
standard. Same powertrain and efficiency as Limited.

Adds over Limited:
- 20" gloss-black alloy wheels
- Black mirror caps and exterior accents
- Blacked-out badging
- Same feature content as Limited otherwise

**Value note:** The Nightshade primarily adds aesthetic differentiation over the Limited. If a
Nightshade and a Limited are similarly priced, the Nightshade offers no meaningful functional
advantage — choose based on color preference.

## Part 5 — What to Exclude

The following listing types are out of scope for this search and should be flagged and excluded
from recommendations:

1. **Any "Hybrid MAX" or "MAX" trim listing** — wrong powertrain. Flag clearly regardless of
   price or mileage.
2. **Any non-hybrid Grand Highlander** — gas-only models (XLE gas, Limited gas, Platinum gas)
   use a 2.4L turbo engine and get 22–24 MPG. Fundamentally different vehicle. Exclude.
3. **Standard Toyota Highlander (non-Grand)** — a completely different, smaller vehicle. If a
   listing appears to be for "Highlander Hybrid" without "Grand" in the name, flag as wrong
   model.
4. **Platinum trim** — the Platinum is exclusively Hybrid MAX powered in all years. If a
   Platinum appears, it is MAX and out of scope.
5. **Any listing over 50,000 miles** — per search parameters.
6. **Any listing older than 2024** — the Grand Highlander did not exist before 2024.

## Part 8 — Evaluation Flags

### Always flag

- **"MAX" or "Hybrid MAX" in trim name:** Out of scope. Exclude and note clearly.
- **Year before 2024:** Wrong model (standard Highlander). Exclude.
- **Platinum trim:** MAX-only powertrain. Out of scope.
- **Gas-only listing:** Not a hybrid. Exclude.
- **2024 model year:** Note the first-year reliability concerns and recommend verifying the side
  airbag recall (NHTSA recall) has been completed via VIN lookup.
- **Mileage over 50,000:** Exceeds search parameter. Exclude.
- **Real-world MPG expectation:** Flag that EPA figures (36 combined) overstate typical
  real-world results. Owners commonly report 27–32 MPG in mixed driving. Do not cite EPA
  figures as though they represent real-world performance.

### Positive signals

- 2025 model year with under 25,000 miles — newer production, lower first-year issue exposure
- Limited trim with AWD under $53,000 — represents strong value for the feature set
- XLE AWD with under 30,000 miles in the $44,000–$47,000 range — near sweet spot pricing
- $0 shipping — Grand Highlanders ship from Toyota's Indiana plant; some Carvana inventory
  may be available locally with no transport fee
- Listings with the bench seat option noted (8-passenger capacity) — adds versatility for
  large families at no additional cost if already configured

### Concern signals

- Priced above $55,000 for a non-Limited trim — likely overpriced vs. market
- Any listing without a clearly identified trim name — request clarification before evaluating
- 2024 model with no mention of recall status in Carvana's vehicle history report
- Mileage that seems very low for age (e.g., a 2024 with 500 miles) priced near new MSRP —
  may not represent a real savings vs. buying new with full warranty intact
- FWD listing in a climate or use case where AWD would be expected — confirm buyer's needs

## Part 9 — Analyst Output Notes

When producing analysis based on this reference document and live Carvana listing data:

1. **Exclude all MAX trim listings immediately.** Do not rank, score, or comment on them beyond
   noting they are out of scope. Do not suggest them as alternatives.
2. **Identify year clearly** and apply the 2024 first-year reliability caveat to every 2024
   listing. Do not apply it to 2025 listings.
3. **Note AWD vs. FWD status** for every XLE listing — this affects both price and MPG.
4. **Do not cite EPA MPG as real-world performance.** Use "EPA-estimated" language and note
   that owner-reported averages are typically 27–32 MPG in mixed driving.
5. **The Nightshade is appearance-only** — do not treat it as a meaningfully superior trim to
   Limited. If priced the same, it is equivalent in function.
6. **Flag the fuel tank underfilling issue** specifically on 2024 listings, as it directly
   affects the practical utility of the vehicle for road trips — which is a primary use case
   for a three-row family SUV.
7. **Give a concrete final recommendation** naming a specific listing (year, trim, AWD/FWD,
   approximate price) with a one-sentence rationale. Prefer 2025 over 2024 if prices are
   comparable. Prefer Limited over XLE if within $3,000–4,000 of each other, given the
   meaningful comfort upgrades.
