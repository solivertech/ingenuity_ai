# Subaru Forester Reference Guide — Carvana Listing Evaluation Context

> **Purpose:** This document provides authoritative reference data for evaluating used Carvana listings
> for the Subaru Forester across the 2021–2025 model years. Use this data to assess whether a given
> listing represents good value, identify the trim level, flag concerns, and contextualize pricing and
> features against known benchmarks.
>
> **Buyer profile:** Located in Phoenix, AZ. Budget up to $30,000. Interested in hybrid trims.
> Financing with approximately $3,000 down at ~7.5% APR over 60 months.

## Part 1 — Model Overview & Reliability

- **Generation in range:** 5th gen (2021–2024 gas models). New 6th gen introduced for 2025, with hybrid available for the first time.
- **Reliability:** Great. J.D. Power scores consistently above average. EyeSight driver assist technology is a class standout.
- **Engines (2021–2024):** 2.5L Boxer 4-cylinder (182 hp). This is the lowest horsepower in this group — can feel underpowered on highway merges.
- **Engine (2025 Hybrid):** 2.5L Boxer + permanent magnet AC synchronous motor (194 hp combined). Toyota-derived series-parallel hybrid system, adapted by Subaru to work with Symmetrical AWD.
- **Gas MPG (2021–2024, all AWD):** 26 city / 33 highway (Wilderness: 25/28)
- **Hybrid MPG (2025):** 35 city / 34 highway combined — lower than RAV4 Hybrid and Sportage Hybrid, but higher than gas Forester.
- **Cargo (behind rear seats):** 29.6 cu ft (smallest in the compact SUV segment by a significant margin)
- **Rear legroom:** ~39.4 inches
- **AWD:** Subaru Symmetrical AWD is standard on every trim at no extra cost — a genuine differentiator. The 2025 Hybrid retains true mechanical AWD (not electrically simulated), which is superior to the RAV4 Hybrid's electronic AWD system in genuine off-road conditions.
- **Ground clearance:** 8.7 inches standard | 9.2 inches on Wilderness trim
- **Towing:** 1,500 lbs (all gas trims except Wilderness: 3,000 lbs)
- **Best value trim (used, gas):** Premium
- **Best value trim (new, hybrid):** Premium Hybrid
- **Primary strength:** AWD standard on every trim. Best visibility in class (upright boxy design, large windows). Best off-road capability at standard trims. EyeSight ADAS is excellent.
- **Notable weakness:** Smallest cargo floor in the group. Lowest horsepower. No hybrid option in 2021–2024. The 2025 Forester Hybrid is first model year — reliability data has not yet accumulated. Infotainment touchscreen on 2025 models has received criticism for slow responsiveness in early owner reviews.

## Part 5 — Evaluation Flags & Red Flags

### Always flag

- **2025 Forester Hybrid:** Flag as first model year. No long-term reliability data. Recommend caution.
- **Mileage vs. age:** Flag if mileage is unusually high for the year. Rule of thumb: more than 15,000 miles per year is above average. More than 20,000 miles per year is high and warrants noting.
- **Shipping cost:** If shipping is listed, add to total price for true cost comparison. Flag when shipping is $0 vs. when it adds cost.

### Positive signals

- Any listing where Carvana's quoted monthly payment aligns with or is lower than the estimated payment at 7.5% APR / 60 months / $3,000 down
- Any hybrid with $0 shipping to Phoenix
- Gas Forester Premium at or below the low end of its range — strong value, AWD standard

### Concern signals

- Price more than 10% above the used market range for that year/trim
- Mileage above 70,000
- Any listing without a VIN (data quality issue — cannot verify history)
- Gas Forester (2021–2024) priced similarly to Forester Hybrid (2025) — the hybrid commands a significant premium for good reason

## Part 6 — Analyst Notes for Output Formatting

When producing analysis based on this reference document and live Carvana listing data:

1. **Always note AWD status** — Symmetrical AWD is standard on every Forester trim; this is a genuine differentiator vs. competitors
2. **Always flag hybrid vs. gas** — 2025 Hybrid is first-year; reliability is unproven. Flag clearly.
3. **Compare estimated monthly payment** against the buyer's financing profile ($3,000 down, 7.5% APR, 60 months) for each listing
4. **Do not treat missing shipping cost as $0** — label it "shipping unavailable" and note it may add to total cost
5. **Rank by value score first**, but provide qualitative commentary that goes beyond the score — a high-scoring listing with a concerning mileage-to-age ratio should have that noted explicitly
6. **Keep the final recommendation concrete** — name a specific listing (year, make, model, trim, price) as the top pick with a one-sentence rationale
7. **Note cargo space limitation** — at 29.6 cu ft, the Forester has significantly less cargo space than CR-V (39.2) and Sportage (39.6); flag if this may matter to the buyer
