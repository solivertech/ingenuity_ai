# Kia Sportage Reference Guide — Carvana Listing Evaluation Context

> **Purpose:** This document provides authoritative reference data for evaluating used Carvana listings
> for the Kia Sportage across the 2021–2025 model years. Use this data to assess whether a given listing
> represents good value, identify the trim level, flag concerns, and contextualize pricing and features
> against known benchmarks.
>
> **Buyer profile:** Located in Phoenix, AZ. Budget up to $30,000. Interested in hybrid trims.
> Financing with approximately $3,000 down at ~7.5% APR over 60 months.

---

## Part 1 — Model Overview & Reliability

- **Generation in range:** 4th gen (2021–2022) and 5th gen (2023–2025). These are very different vehicles.
  - **4th gen (2021–2022): Avoid.** Older platform, smaller interior, less tech, weaker MPG (23/30). Not a bad car but the 2023 redesign is dramatically better in nearly every measurable dimension. The price gap on the used market is rarely worth the tradeoff.
  - **5th gen (2023–2025): Recommended.** Major redesign with class-leading interior tech, the largest cargo space in this group, and the most rear legroom.
- **Reliability:** Good (5th gen). J.D. Power predicted reliability 83/100 ("Great") for 2023–2024 models. Long-term data still accumulating for the newer generation.
- **Engines (5th gen gas):** 2.5L 4-cylinder (187 hp, 8-speed automatic)
- **Engine (5th gen hybrid):** 1.6L turbocharged 4-cylinder + electric motor (227 hp combined) — note this is a different, more powerful engine than the gas model, not the same 2.5L with a motor added.
- **Gas MPG (5th gen):** 25 city / 28 highway — weakest gas MPG in this group.
- **Hybrid MPG (FWD):** 42 city / 44 highway — best EPA numbers in this group
- **Hybrid MPG (AWD):** 38 city / 38 highway
- **Cargo (behind rear seats):** 39.6 cu ft (largest in the compact SUV segment)
- **Rear legroom:** 41.3 inches (most in the compact SUV segment)
- **AWD:** Optional on most gas trims. Optional on hybrid trims. SX trim is FWD only.
- **Towing:** 2,500 lbs (gas, highest in group) | varies by hybrid trim
- **Warranty:** 5-year/60,000-mile basic | 10-year/100,000-mile powertrain (new). For used buyers, powertrain warranty transfers at 5-year/60,000-mile to subsequent owners — still better than Toyota and Honda.
- **Best value trim (used):** EX (gas) | Hybrid EX
- **Primary strength:** Most interior space and rear legroom. Best EPA hybrid MPG. Best standard tech at EX level (12.3" screen, wireless charging standard). Most powerful hybrid powertrain (227 hp).
- **Notable weakness:** Gas model MPG is the worst in this group. 5th gen is still relatively new — less long-term reliability data vs. Honda and Toyota. 4th gen (2021–2022) should be actively avoided.

## Part 5 — Evaluation Flags & Red Flags

### Always flag

- **Kia 2021 or 2022:** Flag as 4th generation. Significantly inferior to 2023+ redesign. Note this clearly.
- **Mileage vs. age:** Flag if mileage is unusually high for the year. Rule of thumb: more than 15,000 miles per year is above average. More than 20,000 miles per year is high and warrants noting.
- **Shipping cost:** If shipping is listed, add to total price for true cost comparison. Flag when shipping is $0 vs. when it adds cost.

### Positive signals

- Sportage Hybrid EX with AWD under $31,000
- Any hybrid with $0 shipping to Phoenix
- Any listing where Carvana's quoted monthly payment is at or below the estimated payment at 7.5% APR / 60 months / $3,000 down

### Concern signals

- Price more than 10% above the used market range for that year/trim
- Mileage above 70,000
- Any listing without a VIN (data quality issue — cannot verify history)
- Kia 4th gen priced similarly to 5th gen (2023+)

## Part 6 — Analyst Notes for Output Formatting

When producing analysis based on this reference document and live Carvana listing data:

1. **Always identify the generation** for Kia Sportage listings (4th gen 2021–2022 vs. 5th gen 2023+)
2. **Always note the AWD status** — optional on most trims; SX (gas) is FWD only; flag clearly
3. **Always note hybrid vs. gas** — hybrid uses a different, more powerful engine (1.6T, 227 hp) vs. gas (2.5L, 187 hp)
4. **Compare estimated monthly payment** against the buyer's financing profile ($3,000 down, 7.5% APR, 60 months) for each listing
5. **Do not treat missing shipping cost as $0** — label it "shipping unavailable" and note it may add to total cost
6. **Rank by value score first**, but provide qualitative commentary that goes beyond the score — a high-scoring listing with a concerning mileage-to-age ratio should have that noted explicitly
7. **Keep the final recommendation concrete** — name a specific listing (year, make, model, trim, price) as the top pick with a one-sentence rationale
8. **Note 4th gen listings clearly** — if a 2021–2022 Sportage appears, explicitly state it is the older generation and quantify the inferior specs vs. 2023+
