# Mazda CX-5 — Carvana Listing Evaluation Context

> **Purpose:** This document provides authoritative reference data for evaluating used Carvana
> listings for the Mazda CX-5. Use this data to identify trim levels accurately, assess value,
> flag concerns, and produce consistent, actionable analysis.
>
> **Search parameters:**
> - Make/Model: Mazda CX-5
> - Years: 2017, 2019, 2020 only (2018 is intentionally excluded)
> - Trims: Touring and Grand Touring only
> - Max price: $20,000
> - Max mileage: 80,000 miles
> - Note: Grand Touring Reserve and Signature trims are out of scope — do not recommend them

## Part 1 — Model Overview

The Mazda CX-5 is a compact two-row crossover SUV widely regarded as one of the most
driver-focused, refined, and premium-feeling vehicles in its class. It is frequently compared
favorably to entry-level luxury SUVs from Audi, Acura, and BMW despite its mainstream price.

**Generation in range:** All three target years (2017, 2019, 2020) are part of the second
generation CX-5, which launched with a full redesign in 2017. The platform and basic powertrain
are shared across this entire range, making cross-year comparisons straightforward.

**Key character traits of the CX-5:**
- Best-in-class driving dynamics and steering feel for the segment
- Premium interior quality with materials that punch well above price point
- Above-average reliability with Mazda's SKYACTIV technology
- Smaller cargo area than most rivals (30.9 cu ft behind rear seats vs. ~39 cu ft in CR-V)
- Smaller rear seat than most rivals — not ideal for rear-facing child seats or very tall adults
- Small fuel tank (15.3 gallons) means more frequent fill-ups than competitors
- Excellent resale value retention

**Why 2018 is excluded from this search:** The buyer has specifically requested 2017, 2019, and
2020 only. Do not suggest or recommend 2018 listings even if they appear in results. Exclude them.

## Part 2 — Critical Year-by-Year Differences

Understanding these differences is essential for accurate listing evaluation.

### 2017

- **Generation:** First year of the second-generation CX-5. Fully redesigned from 2016.
- **Engine:** 2.5L SKYACTIV-G 4-cylinder, 187 hp, 185 lb-ft torque. One engine for all trims.
- **Transmission:** 6-speed automatic only (manual no longer offered)
- **AWD:** Optional on all trims (adds ~$1,300). Not standard on Touring or Grand Touring.
- **Apple CarPlay/Android Auto:** NOT available natively. The 2017 CX-5 does not have
  CarPlay or Android Auto. Mazda offered a dealer-installed retrofit for approximately
  $400–500, but many 2017 vehicles have not had this done. Treat any 2017 listing as
  lacking CarPlay unless confirmed otherwise in listing details.
- **Trim lineup:** Sport, Touring, Grand Select, Grand Touring. Note the "Grand Select" —
  a 2017-only trim that sits between Touring and Grand Touring. See trim section for details.
- **Infotainment:** 7" Mazda Connect touchscreen. The rotary controller interface is the
  primary input — the screen does not function as a touchscreen while the vehicle is moving.
- **Safety:** Forward collision warning with automatic emergency braking standard on all
  trims. Touring adds blind-spot monitoring and rear cross-traffic alert. Grand Touring
  adds adaptive front-lighting (headlights that swivel into corners).
- **NHTSA rating:** 5 stars overall. IIHS Top Safety Pick+.
- **Reliability:** Above average. Fewer complaints than the 2019 model year.

### 2019

- **Engine:** Same 2.5L naturally aspirated 4-cylinder (187 hp) for Sport, Touring, and
  Grand Touring. A turbocharged 2.5L (227–250 hp) was introduced for the Grand Touring
  Reserve and Signature only — not available on the in-scope Touring or Grand Touring trims.
- **AWD:** Optional on Touring and Grand Touring.
- **Apple CarPlay/Android Auto:** Standard on Touring, Grand Touring, Grand Touring Reserve,
  and Signature. NOT available on the base Sport trim. This is the first CX-5 year with
  native CarPlay — a meaningful upgrade over 2017.
- **360-degree camera:** Available on Grand Touring Reserve and Signature only — not on
  Grand Touring. Do not expect surround-view camera on any in-scope 2019 trim.
- **Known concern — cracked cylinder head (2018–2019):** Owner forums have documented
  cases of cracked cylinder heads on 2018–2019 CX-5 models, typically presenting as oil
  leaks, sometimes at relatively low mileage (~25K miles). This issue is not universal and
  many owners have had no problems, but it is worth flagging on 2019 listings. Most cases
  occurred while still under warranty. For a used 2019 listing, recommend checking whether
  this was ever addressed in service records.
- **Infotainment:** Same 7" Mazda Connect system. CarPlay is wired (not wireless).
  Some owners report occasional freezing of the infotainment system — a software update
  typically resolves this.
- **Reliability:** RepairPal 4.5/5, ranked #4 of 32 in midsize SUVs.

### 2020

- **Engine:** Unchanged 2.5L naturally aspirated 4-cylinder (187 hp) for Touring and
  Grand Touring.
- **AWD:** Optional on Touring and Grand Touring.
- **Apple CarPlay/Android Auto:** Standard on Touring and Grand Touring (same as 2019).
- **Changes from 2019:** Quieter cabin due to additional sound deadening. G-Vectoring
  Control Plus (GVC+) added standard to all trims — subtly improves handling stability
  through corner entry and exit via micro engine torque adjustments. Diesel engine
  discontinued. Minor safety feature additions on higher trims.
- **Known concerns:** Infotainment system freezing (especially in first 5,000 miles — a
  software update typically resolves this). Paint chipping easily on the front bumper is
  the most commonly reported cosmetic complaint. Automatic liftgate occasionally opens on
  its own on Grand Touring trims.
- **Reliability:** KBB 4.6/5. Generally considered slightly better than 2019 due to the
  cylinder head concern being less prevalent. Strong owner satisfaction scores.
- **Best year in this search:** The 2020 is the most refined year of the three, with the
  quieter cabin, GVC+, and CarPlay. If price and mileage are comparable across years,
  prefer 2020 over 2019, and 2019 over 2017.

## Part 3 — Trim Level Details

### What is in scope

Only Touring and Grand Touring trims are in scope for this search.

### What is out of scope (flag and exclude)

- **Sport trim:** Base trim. Lacks blind-spot monitoring, heated seats, CarPlay (2017 only).
  Exclude if it appears in results — does not meet trim requirement.
- **Grand Select (2017 only):** A special trim that slots between Touring and Grand Touring.
  It has many Grand Touring features but lacks some safety tech. It is technically neither
  Touring nor Grand Touring. Exclude from recommendations and note it is out of scope.
- **Grand Touring Reserve:** Uses the turbocharged engine, has standard AWD. A much better
  car than the Grand Touring in many respects, but will rarely be found under $20,000 for
  these years, and is out of scope per the trim requirement. If encountered under budget,
  flag as out of scope but note the buyer may wish to reconsider their criteria.
- **Signature:** Top trim with turbo engine. Out of scope. Very unlikely under $20,000.

### Touring Trim

Available: 2017, 2019, 2020

**Standard features across all Touring years:**
- 2.5L SKYACTIV-G engine, 187 hp, 6-speed automatic
- 7" Mazda Connect infotainment with rotary controller
- Blind-spot monitoring with rear cross-traffic alert (adds over Sport)
- Heated front seats
- Dual-zone automatic climate control
- Leather-wrapped steering wheel and shift knob
- Power driver's seat (6-way)
- 17" alloy wheels
- LED headlights with automatic high beams
- Rain-sensing wipers
- Active driving display (heads-up display projected on windshield)
- Rear USB charging ports

**2017 Touring specific:**
- No Apple CarPlay or Android Auto natively
- No power liftgate standard (available as part of Preferred Equipment Package)
- Preferred Equipment Package (optional ~$1,500): adds moonroof, Bose audio, power
  liftgate, navigation — significantly increases value if present on a used listing

**2019–2020 Touring specific:**
- Apple CarPlay and Android Auto standard
- Otherwise largely similar feature set to 2017 Touring

**Touring value note:** A 2019 or 2020 Touring with the Preferred Equipment Package
(moonroof, Bose, power liftgate) represents excellent value and approaches Grand Touring
comfort at a lower price point. Check for this package on Touring listings.

### Grand Touring Trim ⭐ Best Value for Features

Available: 2017, 2019, 2020

**Standard features (adds over Touring):**
- Genuine leather-trimmed seats (vs. cloth on Touring)
- Bose 10-speaker premium audio system (standard, not a package add)
- Power sliding glass moonroof with interior sunshade
- 8-way power-adjustable driver's seat with lumbar support
- Power-adjustable passenger seat
- Heated side mirrors
- LED combination taillights
- LED front fog lights
- Adaptive front-lighting system (headlights swivel into turns)
- Auto-dimming rearview mirror with HomeLink
- Power rear liftgate with programmable height adjustment
- Mazda navigation system (note: navigation on 2017 requires SD card; some used 2017s
  may have a missing or damaged card — the Mazda dealer charges ~$475 CAD for replacement)
- 19" alloy wheels (vs. 17" on Touring)
- Heated front seats (same as Touring)

**2017 Grand Touring specific:**
- No Apple CarPlay or Android Auto natively (same limitation as all 2017 models)
- Grand Touring Premium Package (optional ~$1,830): adds head-up display (active driving
  display is upgraded), heated rear seats, heated steering wheel. If present, significantly
  increases the listing's desirability.

**2019–2020 Grand Touring specific:**
- Apple CarPlay and Android Auto standard
- GT Premium Package (optional ~$1,625 on 2020): adds ventilated front seats,
  heated rear seats, heated steering wheel, traffic sign recognition, head-up display
  upgrade. Highly desirable if found on a used listing.

**Grand Touring identification tips:**
- Leather seats are the clearest visual identifier (Touring has cloth)
- 19" wheels (Touring has 17")
- Power liftgate standard on Grand Touring, optional on Touring
- Bose audio standard on Grand Touring

## Part 4 — Powertrain & Fuel Economy

All in-scope trims (Touring and Grand Touring, 2017/2019/2020) use the same engine.

| Spec | Value |
|------|-------|
| Engine | 2.5L SKYACTIV-G naturally aspirated 4-cylinder |
| Horsepower | 187 hp @ 6,000 rpm |
| Torque | 185–186 lb-ft |
| Transmission | 6-speed automatic |
| Fuel | Regular 87 octane |
| Fuel tank | 15.3 gallons |

**EPA fuel economy:**

| Drivetrain | City | Highway | Combined |
|------------|------|---------|----------|
| FWD | 25 mpg | 31 mpg | 27 mpg |
| AWD | 24 mpg | 30 mpg | 26 mpg |

**Real-world note:** Owners consistently report real-world fuel economy close to the EPA
estimates, often achieving 27–30 mpg in mixed driving. Highway trips at moderate speeds
(65–70 mph) commonly return 32–34 mpg. The small 15.3-gallon tank gives approximately
400–415 miles of range on a full tank FWD, less with AWD.

**AWD system:** Mazda's i-ACTIV AWD is an intelligent system that proactively distributes
torque before wheel slip occurs. It is genuine AWD (not just a stability-control overlay)
and performs well in rain and light snow. However, it is not designed for serious off-road use.

## Part 5 — Dimensions & Cargo

The CX-5 is notably smaller inside than key rivals like the Honda CR-V and Toyota RAV4.
This is relevant context when evaluating value — the CX-5 trades interior volume for
superior driving dynamics and cabin quality.

| Measurement | 2017–2020 CX-5 | Honda CR-V (for reference) |
|---|---|---|
| Cargo behind rear seats | 30.9 cu ft | 39.2 cu ft |
| Cargo max (seats folded) | 59.6 cu ft | 70.9 cu ft |

**Key consideration:** If the buyer needs maximum cargo space or rear seat room for larger
adults or rear-facing child seats, the CX-5 is at a disadvantage vs. most rivals.

## Part 6 — Reliability & Known Issues

### Overall reliability

The second-generation CX-5 (2017+) has above-average reliability. Mazda ranks above
average in J.D. Power dependability surveys. RepairPal rates the CX-5 4.5/5 and ranks it
#4 of 32 among midsize SUVs. The SKYACTIV engine and 6-speed transmission are proven and
durable — owners regularly report 150,000–200,000+ miles with routine maintenance.

### Issues to be aware of

**Cracked cylinder head (primarily 2018–2019):**
The most significant mechanical concern documented in this era. Some 2018–2019 CX-5 owners
reported cracked cylinder heads presenting as oil leaks, occasionally at low mileage. The
2017 and 2020 are less affected. For 2019 listings, note this concern in the analysis and
recommend verifying service records show no history of oil leaks or cylinder head work.

**Infotainment system freezing (2019–2020):**
The most common complaint. The Mazda Connect system can freeze or require a reboot,
especially when new. A software update from the dealer typically resolves the issue. This
is a nuisance concern, not a reliability red flag.

**Paint quality:**
Multiple owners across years report paint that chips easily, especially on the front bumper
and hood leading edge. This is a cosmetic concern that should be noted when evaluating
used listings — check the vehicle history for any paint/bodywork claims.

**Navigation SD card (2017 Grand Touring):**
The 2017 Grand Touring uses an SD card for navigation maps. Replacement cards are
expensive (~$475 at dealer). If a 2017 Grand Touring listing does not mention the SD card,
it may be missing. This does not affect CarPlay functionality (if retrofitted) but is worth
noting.

**AWD system clunk:**
A small number of owners across years have reported clunking sounds from the AWD system,
typically related to the rear differential. This is relatively rare but worth noting during
a test drive if applicable.

**Positive reliability markers:**
- Oil changes every 7,500 miles (Mazda's longer interval reflects engine durability)
- Timing chain (not belt) — no scheduled replacement
- 6-speed automatic is a known-reliable unit with no widespread complaints

## Part 8 — Evaluation Flags

### Always flag

- **2018 year:** Excluded from this search. Flag and exclude any 2018 listing.
- **Sport trim:** Out of scope. Exclude.
- **Grand Select (2017):** Out of scope. Exclude and note it is a 2017-only special trim.
- **Grand Touring Reserve or Signature:** Out of scope. Flag clearly.
- **2017 listings without CarPlay:** Note the absence as a genuine usability concern for
  buyers who rely on smartphone navigation and audio. Retrofitting CarPlay costs ~$400–500
  at a Mazda dealer. Factor this into value assessment.
- **2019 listings with oil leak history or cylinder head work:** Flag prominently. This is
  the most significant mechanical concern for 2019 models. Recommend buyer verify via VIN
  history check.
- **Mileage over 70,000:** Within the search limit (80,000) but flag anything over 70K as
  higher mileage for a vehicle in this price range. Note that the SKYACTIV engine is built
  for longevity, but routine maintenance history becomes more important at this stage.
- **Missing navigation SD card (2017 Grand Touring):** If listing notes navigation but the
  SD card situation is unclear, flag the potential $400+ replacement cost.

### Positive signals

- 2019 or 2020 Touring with the Preferred Equipment Package (moonroof, Bose, power
  liftgate) — approaches Grand Touring comfort at lower trim price
- 2020 Grand Touring FWD with 65,000–79,000 miles under $20,000 — best combination of
  refinement and features within budget
- 2019 Grand Touring FWD with under 60,000 miles near $19,000 — strong value
- AWD listing priced within $1,000 of a comparable FWD listing — AWD at near-parity is
  good value
- 2017 Grand Touring with GT Premium Package (heated rear seats, heated steering wheel,
  head-up display) confirmed present — adds significant comfort value
- Any listing with confirmed low-mileage relative to year (e.g., 2019 with under 40,000
  miles at or near $20,000 — rare but worth highlighting prominently)

### Concern signals

- Price near or above $20,000 for a 2017 model — likely overpriced vs. market
- High mileage (70,000+) on a 2019 or 2020 — acceptable but warrants noting
- FWD listing priced the same as AWD equivalent — may indicate other issues or
  inaccurate listing data
- Any listing describing paint damage, front bumper damage, or prior bodywork — ties
  to the known paint fragility issue
- 2019 listing with any mention of oil consumption, oil leaks, or engine warning lights
  in the vehicle history — cylinder head concern

## Part 9 — Analyst Output Notes

When producing analysis for this search:

1. **Prioritize year when scoring.** All else being equal, rank 2020 > 2019 > 2017 due to
   refinements, CarPlay availability, and reliability profile.

2. **Always note CarPlay status for 2017 listings.** It is absent natively. Do not assume
   it has been retrofitted. If the listing does not mention it, treat it as absent and note
   the ~$400–500 retrofit cost as a consideration.

3. **Always note FWD vs. AWD.** State clearly which configuration each listing is and
   factor the AWD premium into value scoring. Do not assume AWD is present — many CX-5s
   in this price range are FWD.

4. **Flag the Grand Select trim (2017) immediately.** It is neither Touring nor Grand
   Touring and does not meet the search criteria. Exclude it without ranking.

5. **Note package presence when identifiable.** A Touring with the Preferred Equipment
   Package or a Grand Touring with the GT Premium Package is meaningfully more valuable
   than a base version of the same trim. Highlight this when the listing indicates the
   package is present.

6. **The cargo space limitation is worth one note** in the overall analysis — the CX-5 is
   smaller inside than most compact SUV rivals. Mention it once as context, not repeatedly.

7. **Give a concrete final recommendation** naming a specific listing by year, trim, FWD
   or AWD, approximate price, and mileage with a one-sentence rationale. If multiple strong
   listings exist, identify the top pick and one runner-up.

8. **The $20,000 budget is hard.** Do not recommend any listing above $20,000. If all
   listings exceed the budget, flag this clearly and identify the closest listing to the
   budget rather than ranking out-of-budget options.
